# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the IliaGPT community pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- Using welcoming and inclusive language in discussions, code reviews, and documentation
- Recognizing and crediting contributors for their work and ideas
- Asking questions thoughtfully and responding to questions with patience
- Supporting newer contributors and helping them get oriented in the codebase
- Raising concerns and disagreements through constructive, civil dialogue

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or electronic address, without their explicit permission (doxxing)
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Sustained disruption of community discussions, events, or contributions
- Dismissing or attacking someone because of their skill level or background
- Making negative generalizations about groups of people
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

Community leaders will act with consistency and fairness across all reports. The same standards apply to everyone, including maintainers and project leads.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official IliaGPT email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- The IliaGPT GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, commit messages)
- The IliaGPT Discord server and any other community chat platforms
- The IliaGPT community forums and mailing lists
- IliaGPT-affiliated events, meetups, and conferences (virtual or in-person)
- Direct messages between community members when the context is related to IliaGPT
- Any public space where a community member is acting as a representative of IliaGPT

This Code of Conduct does **not** apply to interactions that are entirely unrelated to IliaGPT and occur in spaces not affiliated with the project.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**Email:** conduct@iliagpt.io

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

When submitting a report, please include:

- Your contact information (name and email)
- Names or usernames of any individuals involved
- A description of the incident, including dates and locations
- Links to any publicly available material (GitHub issues, Discord messages, etc.)
- Any additional context or evidence you believe is relevant

You will receive acknowledgment of your report within 48 hours.

### What Happens After You Report

1. The report is received and acknowledged within 48 hours.
2. The enforcement team reviews the report and may contact you for additional information.
3. A decision on enforcement action is made within 14 days for most cases.
4. The enforcement action is applied and you are informed of the outcome.
5. If the reported party wishes to appeal, they may do so by emailing conduct@iliagpt.io with "Appeal" in the subject line within 7 days of the decision.

All enforcement discussions are conducted confidentially. The names of reporters are not shared with the accused party unless required by law.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Community Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals; or a single severe incident such as doxxing a community member or making credible threats of violence.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

---

## Appeals

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error, or if you are the subject of an enforcement action and wish to appeal:

1. Send an email to conduct@iliagpt.io with "Appeal" in the subject line within 7 days of the decision.
2. Include your reasoning and any additional context or evidence.
3. A different member of the enforcement team who was not involved in the original decision will review the appeal.
4. You will receive a final decision within 14 days of the appeal being submitted.
5. Appeal decisions are final.

---

## Privacy

All reports and enforcement actions are handled confidentially. The enforcement team will not disclose the identity of reporters except where required by law. Enforcement discussions are not made public, though the outcome may be announced in general terms if it is in the community's interest to do so (for example, banning a participant at a public event).
